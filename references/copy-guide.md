# Pharmaceutical/Scientific Brand Copy Guide

## Brand Voice Principles

### Core Tone: Scientific Authority + Accessible Precision
- Speak like a trusted research partner, not a sales pitch
- Use precise language without being inaccessible
- Confidence without arrogance; expertise without jargon overload

### Tone Pillars
1. **Authoritative** — We know our science deeply
2. **Trustworthy** — Every claim is verifiable
3. **Premium** — Quality speaks through specificity
4. **Clinical** — Exact, measured, professional
5. **Progressive** — Forward-thinking research community

---

## Copy Frameworks by Section

### Hero Headlines
Format: [Scientific Superlative] + [Benefit/Purpose]

Examples:
- "Research-Grade Peptides. Uncompromising Purity."
- "Engineered for Scientists. Trusted by Researchers Worldwide."
- "Precision Synthesized. Independently Verified. Research Ready."
- "The Standard for Peptide Research Excellence."
- "Where Laboratory Precision Meets Research Ambition."

Avoid:
- "Best peptides ever!" (hyperbolic, unscientific)
- "Transform your body!" (crosses into medical claim territory)
- "Buy now for huge discounts!" (cheapens brand)

### Hero Subheadlines
1-2 sentences. Lead with credential, end with invitation.

Examples:
- "Every compound synthesized to ≥99% purity, verified by independent HPLC analysis. Explore our research catalog."
- "Trusted by 10,000+ researchers across 45 countries. Discover why precision matters."

### Product Names
Keep original names. Add only:
- Purity designation: "[Name] · ≥99% Purity"
- Research grade indicator: "Research Grade"
- Molecular shorthand where accurate

### Product Descriptions
Structure:
1. **Opening**: What it is + primary research application (1 sentence)
2. **Research context**: Why researchers use this compound (2-3 sentences)
3. **Specifications**: Purity, form, storage, stability
4. **Compliance note**: "For research purposes only. Not for human consumption."

Tone: Like a knowledgeable colleague explaining to a fellow researcher. Not a product page. A data sheet.

Example:
> BPC-157 is a synthetic pentadecapeptide fragment derived from body protection compound, primarily studied for its role in angiogenesis and tissue repair models. Research has explored its activity in gastrointestinal, musculoskeletal, and neurological contexts, with particular interest in its interaction with growth hormone receptor pathways. Available as lyophilized powder with ≥99% purity confirmed by HPLC and mass spectrometry. Store at -20°C, stable for 24 months under proper conditions.

### Trust Signals
Short, specific, verifiable:
- "≥99% Purity — HPLC Verified"
- "Third-Party Tested — COA Available"
- "GMP-Certified Manufacturing"
- "ISO 9001 Compliant Facility"
- "Same-Day Dispatch (Orders Before 2PM)"
- "Tracked & Insured International Shipping"

Avoid vague claims:
- "Best quality" ✗
- "Pure and safe" ✗ (safety claims require regulatory approval)

### Category Descriptions
One sharp sentence per category. Lead with application context.

Examples:
- **Growth Factors**: "Compounds studied in cellular proliferation, regeneration, and signaling research."
- **Cognitive Research**: "Nootropic peptides explored in memory, focus, and neuroprotection models."
- **Anti-Aging Research**: "Telomere, mitochondrial, and senolytic research compounds."
- **Performance Research**: "Compounds studied in metabolic regulation, lean tissue, and endurance models."

### CTA Button Copy
Primary actions:
- "Shop Research Catalog"
- "Explore Products"
- "View Full Catalog"
- "Add to Cart" (product level)
- "Order Now"

Secondary actions:
- "View Certificate of Analysis"
- "Download COA"
- "Learn More"
- "Read Research"

Avoid:
- "Buy Now and Save!" (discount-first messaging undercuts premium positioning)
- "Get Yours Today!" (too casual for scientific audience)

### Testimonial Format
> "[Quote in their words — specific about outcome or experience]"
> — **[First Name L.]**, [Title/Role], [Institution type if applicable]

Example:
> "Consistent purity across every batch. The COA documentation has been invaluable for our lab's compliance requirements."
> — **Dr. M. Torres**, Research Biochemist, University Laboratory

### Legal/Compliance Footer (always include)
> "All products are intended for research purposes only. Not for human consumption. Not for veterinary use. For use by qualified researchers in laboratory settings. [Brand Name] makes no therapeutic or medical claims. Products have not been evaluated by the FDA or equivalent regulatory bodies."

## Words to Use vs. Avoid

### Use:
synthesized, purity, lyophilized, peptide, compound, research, laboratory, analysis, HPLC, verified, certificate, specification, precision, clinical, molecular, sequence, formulation, independent, validated

### Avoid:
cure, treat, heal, safe for consumption, bodybuilding (if trying to position as research-only), miracle, guaranteed results, FDA approved (unless actually true), pharmaceutical-grade (unless certified)

---

## International Copy Considerations
If serving global markets:
- Use metric measurements (mg, mcg, ml) as primary
- Avoid US-centric idioms
- Currency: show local where possible, USD as fallback
- Shipping: reference international tracking, customs documentation
